Reflection

Questions

1. What have I learned? What do I need to reinforce? What strategies will I follow to improve?

Throughout this course I have learned new and better ways to communicate in a simpler and more casual way in an English conversation. What I need to reinforce is speaking because I pause a lot and it doesn't sound fluent. As strategies I will start having more real life conversations in English.


2. What activity did I like most/least?

I liked the activities where I had to speak to describe a situation or just have a conversation in English because that is what I need most. The activity that I liked the least was the long readings.


3. What activity let me learn more/less?

The activity where we have to speak in English because it focuses you on a common situation of everyday life.


4. How can I use this knowledge in real life?

Reading articles or any reading that interests you, but that is only in English; if you want to see a movie that is only in English and without subtitles or simply if you want to communicate with someone from another country who speaks English.


5. Did I learn something that surprised me, made me reflect, reconsider ideas? (justify).

Yes, I learned quite a few new concepts and words that helped me reflect on the use of new expressions to be able to communicate better.


6. In general, what do I feel about English language?

I feel that it is quite important that people know English because many doors can be opened when you learn to communicate with other people to express what you think or want.
